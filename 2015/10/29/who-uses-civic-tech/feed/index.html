We pivoted to tech + games. This Governing article is a good summary. http://www.governing.com/topics/urban/gov-cards-against-urbanity.html Bottom line – (1) Reliance on the public meeting + powerpoint kills communications and (2) basic knowledge on what programs are and how they work essential

Hope this helps. We are still watching how the allocation of resources is playing out.

]]> By: ejaxon https://civic.io/2015/10/29/who-uses-civic-tech/#comment-10830 Thu, 17 Mar 2016 18:13:23 +0000 http://civic.io/?p=3999#comment-10830 David Shaeffer’s comment echoes one of my thoughts. Much of civic tech is driven by technologists who over-focus on the product itself and neglect all the other parts of the process. Even when civic tech is volunteer-driven and free, we need to think about marketing and ongoing communication with users (as well as how we maintain the product going forward). Another question that arose for me re-reading the study just now is the extent to which sites (esp in the US) are optimized or not for use on mobile. Aside from a few comments, that data wasn’t provided in the discussion, but may be relevant to under-representation of younger people and non-whites in the US.

Finally, one of the things that worries me generally in civic tech is the danger of leaving behind all of the smaller places, not only rural towns and counties, but also smaller suburbs in urban areas. In my area, for example, “Asheville” has an area population of over 300K, but only 80K are in the city itself – the majority live in other jurisdictions, all of them with much smaller populations. And of course folks living in the surrounding, mostly rural counties are even worse off. Civic tech is these places hard to measure because we can’t get big enough samples of users from them, and they’re underrepresented in civic tech generally because they lack the resources, local developer communities, etc. I see figuring out ways to bring them along and to understand the particular issues they face as one of the important challenges we need to be looking at.
