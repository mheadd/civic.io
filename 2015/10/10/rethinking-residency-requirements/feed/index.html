]]> By: bensheldonSheldon https://civic.io/2015/10/10/rethinking-residency-requirements/#comment-10342 Sat, 10 Oct 2015 16:57:15 +0000 http://civic.io/?p=3966#comment-10342 This is a great topic for discussion!

I do think you’re not describing the benefits that residency requirements have on pushing government to be equitable and forward-thinking. Rather than focusing on the struggle of individuals to come into compliance, I’d like to know “why couldn’t the government hire that role from within its current residents?”. I think every outside hire should be viewed as a failure to develop local self-sufficiency.

You see this failure of local self-sufficiency playing out, for example, in urban housing crises in which teachers cannot afford to live within the urban boundaries. This is not a failing of the teachers, but rather a failing of the government to not provide (either/both) equitable salaries or develop affordable housing.

For tech workers, I fear that relaxing residency requirements looks like carpet-baggery: reinforcing tech as privileged outsiders and agents of an extractive economy. Would it be a bridge to develop a local base up which residency requirements could be reinstated, or is a beach-head to relaxing the requirement for other (privileged) positions?
